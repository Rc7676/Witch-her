1 Game Overview & Objective

▼

The Witcher: Old World is a competitive adventure board game for 1–5 players, designed by Łukasz Woźniak and published by Go On Board (2022). It is set during the Golden Age of Witchers — long before the events of the novels or video games — when monster activity was at its peak and all the witcher schools were still thriving.

Objective

Each player controls one witcher and competes to earn the most Victory Points (VP) by the time the game ends. Points come from defeating monsters, completing quests, leveling up your witcher, and collecting trophy tokens.

Core Loop

Each turn you spend 2 action tokens to travel the map, fight monsters, buy new cards, meditate to heal, and complete quests. Combat is resolved using your personal card deck plus custom combat dice, and over the course of the game you improve your deck and your witcher's abilities to tackle ever-tougher monsters.

💡 Note on this guide: Stats, exact card effects, and specific perk choices vary by edition and character. Always defer to your physical rulebook and character boards for exact values — this guide covers how all the systems work together.

2 Components

▼

Board & Map Tiles

  • 1 main game board (map of the Northern Kingdoms region)
  • Modular location tiles — the number placed depends on player count
  • Monster trail track (part of the board)
  • Round / game-end tracker

Cards

  • 5 unique witcher starter decks (one per character)
  • Common action card deck (shared pool for purchasing)
  • Monster cards (one per monster on the trail)
  • Monster reaction decks (one deck per monster type)
  • Exploration / event cards (per location tile)
  • Quest / contract cards

Tokens & Markers

  • Gold coins (1 and 3 denominations)
  • Shield tokens
  • Trophy tokens (one type per monster category)
  • Potion tokens (various types)
  • Mutagen tokens
  • Action tokens (2 per player)
  • Damage / HP tokens
  • Witcher level markers

Miniatures / Standees

  • Witcher miniatures or standees (one per player)
  • Monster standees or miniatures

Dice

  • Custom combat dice (unique symbol faces for the board game)

3 Setup

▼
1
Board: Lay out the main board. Add the correct number of location tiles as shown in the rulebook for your player count (fewer tiles for 2 players, all tiles for 5).
2
Monster Trail: Shuffle the monster cards and populate the trail spaces as directed by the setup diagram. Reveal the starting monsters.
3
Common Card Row: Shuffle the common action card deck. Deal 6 cards face-up to form the purchasable row. Place the remainder as the draw pile next to it.
4
Quest Cards: Shuffle the quest deck and place cards on the designated quest slots on the board, face-up.
5
Choose Witchers: Each player picks a witcher character and takes their character board, miniature/standee, and unique starter deck. Place your witcher on the starting location shown on your board.
6
Starter Decks: Shuffle your 12-card starter deck and draw your starting hand (5 cards).
7
Starting Resources: Take the starting gold and any starting tokens (potions, etc.) shown on your character board.
8
Markers: Place your level marker on Level 1 of your character board. Set the round marker to Round 1.
9
First Player: Give the first-player token to the starting player (use any agreed method). Play proceeds clockwise.
💡 For your first game, use the recommended introductory setup in the rulebook — it selects specific quests and a simplified board configuration to ease new players in.

4 Witcher Characters

▼

The base game includes five witchers — four from the Wolf School and one from the Griffin School. Each has a unique 12-card starter deck and a special ability printed on their character board. All witchers start at the same base stats (check your rulebook for exact starting HP and hand size).

🐺

Geralt of Rivia

Wolf School
Unique ability printed on character board. Starter deck is balanced between attack, defense, and sign options — the most versatile of the five.
▶ Recommended for new players. Balanced playstyle suits any strategy. Works well with both combat-heavy and sign-heavy purchased card builds.
🐺

Vesemir

Wolf School
Unique ability printed on character board. As the eldest and most experienced witcher, his ability reflects a support and teaching role. Starter deck emphasizes reliable, steady combat.
▶ Good for players who enjoy a supporting role and steady VP accumulation. Particularly strong in 3–5 player games.
🐺

Lambert

Wolf School
Unique ability printed on character board. Starter deck leans heavily offensive — more sword-generating cards than the other Wolf witchers.
▶ Aggressive hunter. Best for players who want to race through monsters fast and dominate the trail. Requires careful HP management.
🐺

Eskel

Wolf School
Unique ability printed on character board. Starter deck has a stronger defensive profile relative to the other Wolf witchers, with solid shield generation.
▶ Reliable and durable. Good for players who prefer consistent output over high-variance strategies. Handles difficult fights without needing to flee.
🦅

Coen

Griffin School
Unique ability printed on character board. As the only Griffin School witcher in the base game, his starter deck contains more sign cards than any Wolf witcher.
▶ Signs specialist. Strongest when you invest in sign cards from the common row. Higher skill ceiling — best for players comfortable with the game's deck-building aspect.

Expansion Characters

Expansions and Kickstarter add-ons introduce characters from additional schools, including representatives of the Cat, Bear, Viper, and Manticore schools, as well as special characters from the broader Witcher universe. Each brings a unique starter deck and character ability matching their school's identity.

5 Witcher Schools

▼

A witcher's school defines their combat style, the composition of their starter deck, and their general strategic identity. The base game includes Wolf and Griffin; other schools appear in expansions.

🐺

Wolf School Base Game

The most adaptable school. Starter decks are balanced across attack, defense, and signs. Wolf witchers can pivot toward any specialization as they build their decks. Four characters in the base game.

🦅

Griffin School Base Game

Emphasizes signs above all else. Griffin witchers begin with more sign cards and have abilities that reward playing signs frequently. Build toward a sign-heavy deck for maximum effectiveness.

🐱

Cat School Expansion

Fast and aggressive. Starter decks prioritize offense over defense. Cat witchers aim to kill monsters quickly before sustaining too much damage. High risk, high reward.

🐻

Bear School Expansion

Tough and enduring. Heavy defense emphasis. Bear witchers can withstand prolonged fights and tackle monsters other witchers would flee from. Slower VP accumulation but reliable.

🐍

Viper School Expansion

Assassination and contracts. Viper witchers have unique mechanics that reward taking on witcher-vs-witcher duels alongside monster hunting. Versatile threat on the board.

🦂

Manticore School Expansion

Alchemy specialists. Manticore witchers have expanded potion mechanics and more alchemical synergy in their starter decks. Rewards players who plan their resource use carefully.

6 Round Structure

▼
Phase 1

Player Turns

Starting with the first player and going clockwise, each player takes their full turn before the next. On your turn you spend your 2 action tokens (one per action). After spending both tokens, your turn ends and the next player goes. Free actions (triggered by cards or character abilities) may be taken at any point during your turn without spending an action token.

Phase 2

End of Round

After all players have taken their turns, the end-of-round sequence occurs:

  • Monster Trail: All monsters on the trail advance one space toward the end
  • Trail Refill: A new monster card is revealed and placed at the start of the trail
  • Common Row: Refresh the purchasable card row as instructed (discard remaining cards and deal replacements, or just refill empty spaces — check rulebook)
  • First Player Token: Pass to the next player clockwise
  • Round Marker: Advance the round tracker by 1
  • Reset Action Tokens: All players receive their 2 action tokens back

End of Your Turn

  • Discard all cards played this turn to your personal discard pile
  • Discard cards from your hand down to your hand limit (shown on your character board)
  • Draw cards from your personal deck up to your hand limit
  • If your deck runs out while drawing, shuffle your discard pile into a new deck and continue drawing

7 Actions

▼

On your turn you have 2 action tokens. Spend one token per action from the list below. You may take the same action twice if you wish.

🚶

Travel

Move your witcher to an adjacent location connected by a path on the map. You may also play certain cards during this action to travel additional spaces. The number of spaces you can move and any card requirements depend on the path — check your rulebook for your edition's exact movement rules.

🔍

Explore

At a location that has an exploration deck, draw and resolve the top exploration card. These cards trigger encounters, grant resources, advance quests, or spawn monsters. Once all cards in a location's exploration deck are gone, no further exploration is possible there.

⚔️

Fight a Monster

Initiate combat with a monster present at your current location. This is the primary way to earn trophy tokens and VP. See Section 11 for the full combat rules.

📚

Train (Purchase a Card)

Pay the gold cost shown on a card in the common row to acquire it. The card goes directly to your discard pile (not your hand). The common row is immediately refilled from the draw pile. You may train more than once per turn — each purchase spends one action token.

🌙

Meditate

Discard any number of cards from your hand and heal HP equal to the number of cards discarded (up to your maximum HP). You also trigger your character's unique meditation bonus shown on their board. See Section 17 for full meditation rules.

📜

Take / Complete a Quest

At a location with a quest slot, spend an action to pick up a quest card (place it in front of you) or to complete a quest you already hold (if its conditions are met). Completing a quest grants the reward printed on the card.

⚔️

Challenge a Witcher (Duel)

If another witcher is at your location, spend an action to challenge them to a duel. See Section 20 for duel rules. The challenged witcher cannot refuse.

Free Actions

Certain effects do not cost an action token and can be used at any point during your turn:

  • Playing a card with a "Free Action" keyword
  • Using a potion token you are holding
  • Activating a character ability with a free-action symbol
  • Discarding a card to trigger its "When Discarded" effect

8 Movement & the Map

▼

Map Structure

The game board shows a region of the Northern Kingdoms divided into locations connected by paths. One Travel action moves your witcher along one path to an adjacent location. Some paths may have additional requirements shown on the board — consult your rulebook for your specific edition.

Multiple Witchers at One Location

Any number of witchers may occupy the same location simultaneously. This opens up opportunities for cooperation (joining the same monster fight) and conflict (dueling for VP). Witchers do not block each other's movement.

Location Types

IconTypeWhat You Can Do There
🏙️City / TownTrain (buy cards from the common row), accept or complete quests
🏰Witcher Fortress / School BaseEnhanced meditation, access to witcher-specific resources
🌲Wilderness Path / ForestExplore (draw from the location's event deck), encounter monsters
⚔️Monster LairFight the monster present; possible exploration events
📜Notice BoardPick up or complete quest cards; may allow trophy exchange
💡 Plan your route to combine actions efficiently — a city that is also near active monsters lets you train and fight in consecutive turns without wasting movement.

9 Exploration

▼

Spend an Explore action at a location with an exploration deck to draw and immediately resolve its top card. Exploration is the primary way to discover potions, mutagen fragments, and quest clues away from cities.

🎁 Discovery

Gain a resource immediately: gold, potion tokens, trophy tokens, cards, or mutagen fragments. The card specifies exactly what you receive.

⚡ Event / Encounter

Resolve a short encounter. Usually presents a choice or a simple test — for example, spend a resource to avoid a penalty, or take a risk for a reward. Effects are fully described on the card.

👹 Ambush Monster

A monster appears! You must fight it immediately using the normal combat rules. If you defeat it you gain a trophy; if you flee you suffer the flee penalty. The ambush monster is separate from the trail — it does not appear on the trail track.

📜 Quest Clue

Advances an active quest you hold, moving it closer to completion. Collecting enough clues for a quest may unlock the ability to complete it at the relevant location.

💡 Exploration decks are finite and are not reshuffled. Once exhausted, that location can no longer be explored. Prioritize exploration early in the game when decks are full.

10 Training (Buying Cards)

▼

The Common Card Row

Six cards are always available face-up in a central row, each with a gold cost in the corner. To train, spend a Train action and pay a card's gold cost to acquire it — the card goes to your discard pile immediately. The row is refilled from the draw pile right away. You may buy more than one card per turn (each purchase costs one action).

Card Categories in the Common Row

CategoryFunction
⚔️ Combat CardsGenerate sword (⚔) symbols or dice rolls during combat — your primary damage source
🛡️ Defense CardsGenerate shield (🛡) symbols to block monster damage
🌀 Sign CardsTrigger sign effects (Aard, Igni, Quen, Yrden, Axii) during or outside combat — see Section 13
💰 Economy CardsGenerate gold when played — used to fund further purchases during the same turn
🃏 Special CardsPowerful one-time or situational effects unique to that card

Deck Thinning — Removing Cards

Some cards have an effect that lets you permanently remove a card from your deck (place it out of the game). Removing weak starter cards makes your deck smaller and faster-cycling, so you draw your best cards more often. This is one of the most powerful strategies in the game — prioritize it after purchasing your first few upgrades.

💡 Gold generated by playing cards only lasts until the end of your current turn — it cannot be saved. Spend it all or let it go.

11 Monster Combat

▼

Combat is resolved using your personal card deck and custom combat dice against the monster's own reaction deck. It continues over multiple rounds until the monster is slain or you flee.

1
Initiate: Spend a Fight action. Take the monster's card — it shows HP, level, the trophy type, and which reaction deck to use.
2
Monster Reaction: Flip the top card of the monster's reaction deck. This card shows what the monster does this round: its attack value and any special ability it activates.
3
Play Your Cards: Play as many cards as you wish from your hand face-up, resolving their effects. Cards generate ⚔ (sword) symbols, 🛡 (shield) symbols, gold, card draws, sign effects, or instruct you to roll combat dice.
4
Roll Dice: If any played cards instruct you to roll dice, roll those dice now and add the results (⚔, 🛡, or special symbols) to your running total from cards.
5
Resolve Damage:
  • Your total ⚔ = damage dealt to the monster (reduce its HP)
  • Monster's attack value − your total 🛡 = damage to your HP
  • Both apply simultaneously — you do not need to kill the monster before being hurt
6
Check Status: If the monster's HP is 0 or less → you win (go to step 8). If your HP is 0 → you are knocked out. Otherwise, begin the next combat round (return to step 2 with a new reaction card).
7
New Round Hand: At the start of each new combat round you draw cards back up to your hand limit from your personal deck (cycle the deck if needed). You do not discard cards between combat rounds — you carry your hand over.
8
Victory: Take the trophy token for this monster type. Collect the gold reward shown on the monster card. Consider leveling up if eligible (Section 16). The monster is removed from its location.

Fleeing Combat

At the end of any combat round, before drawing the next reaction card, you may flee. You immediately take the flee penalty shown on the monster card (typically a set amount of HP loss). Your turn then ends. The monster stays at its location with its current HP — another witcher may fight it later in its wounded state.

Being Knocked Out (0 HP)

If your HP drops to 0 during combat: lose any gold you are currently carrying (place it on the board space for others to collect). Reset your HP to the value shown on your character board for knockouts. Your witcher is moved to the nearest friendly location (Kaer Morhen or your school's base). You do not lose cards from your deck.

Monster Reaction Deck

Each monster type has its own reaction deck of cards that define how it behaves. Reaction cards vary in attack value and may include special effects specific to that monster. When a monster's reaction deck runs out, shuffle it and continue. Learning a monster's common reaction cards through experience is a key skill.

12 Card System

▼

Your Personal Deck

Each witcher maintains their own private deck. It starts as your 12-card starter deck and grows as you buy cards from the common row. The deck at any given time consists of all your cards not currently in your hand or discard pile (and not permanently removed from the game).

Hand Limit & Card Draw

Your hand limit is shown on your character board. At the end of every turn, discard all played cards and excess hand cards, then draw back up to your hand limit. During combat, some cards let you draw additional cards beyond your hand limit for that combat round.

Card Anatomy

13 Signs

▼

The five witcher signs — Aard, Igni, Quen, Yrden, and Axii — appear in The Witcher: Old World as card types you can add to your deck. Sign cards are purchased from the common row like any other card and are played during combat to produce their stated board-game effect. Their effects in this board game are distinct from the video games and are fully printed on each individual card.

🌊

Aard

A force-based sign. Aard cards in the board game provide combat advantages relating to disrupting the enemy — exact effect is printed on the card. Generally favors offense.

🔥

Igni

A fire-based sign. Igni cards provide aggressive combat bonuses. Generally one of the highest-damage sign types when built around. Exact value is on the card.

🛡️

Quen

A protective sign. Quen cards provide defensive effects in combat — typically generating shield symbols or persistent protection. Exact effect is on the card.

🕸️

Yrden

A trap-based sign. Yrden cards create tactical advantages in combat, often manipulating how the monster behaves or providing conditional bonuses. Exact effect is on the card.

💫

Axii

A charm-based sign. Axii cards tend to provide control or utility effects that alter combat dynamics. Also useful in witcher-vs-witcher duels. Exact effect is on the card.

Signs and the Griffin School

Coen (Griffin School) is the base-game character most suited to sign-based strategies. His character board and starter deck are oriented around playing sign cards frequently. Sign cards from the common row come in multiple tiers — basic signs are cheaper but weaker; advanced sign cards cost more and produce stronger effects.

💡 Signs are most powerful when you build your deck to include multiple copies of the same sign type, as many cards have bonuses that trigger when you play the same sign more than once per combat round.

14 Potions

▼

Potions are single-use tokens that provide immediate effects. You hold them on your character board in your potion slots (the number of slots is shown on your board and can increase as you level up). Using a potion is a free action — it costs no action token. Exact effect values are printed on each potion token in your edition.

PotionGeneral CategoryWhen Used
🟢 SwallowHealing — restores HPAny time on your turn
⚡ ThunderboltOffense — boosts sword (⚔) output this combat roundDuring combat
🐱 CatPerception — provides a card-draw or scouting benefitDuring combat or exploration
🦉 Tawny OwlStamina — provides an action-related benefitYour turn
🍯 White HoneyCleansing — removes negative status effects or allows additional potion useAny time
❄️ BlizzardPerception — enhances card play during combatDuring combat
🌕 Full MoonMutagenic — grants mutagen-related benefitsYour turn

Gaining Potions

Potions are acquired through: exploration events, quest rewards, specific city actions, and certain card effects. They are stored as tokens on your character board. You cannot hold more potions than your current potion slot count allows — excess potions are discarded.

Potion Slots

Your starting number of potion slots is printed on your character board. Slots can increase when you choose specific level-up perks. The Manticore School (expansion) starts with more potion slots than other schools.

💡 Always enter a major fight with at least one healing potion. A Swallow used at the right moment can be the difference between winning and being knocked out, losing all your carried gold.

15 Quests & Contracts

▼

How Quests Work

Quest cards sit face-up on designated locations on the board. To accept a quest, visit that location and spend a Take Quest action. The card is placed in front of you. You may hold a limited number of quests simultaneously (shown on your character board — typically 2). To complete a quest, its listed conditions must be fulfilled and you must spend a Complete Quest action (often requires visiting a specific location).

Quest Types

⚔️ Monster Contract

Defeat one or more monsters of a specific type or level. Track kills on the quest card (place markers). Return to complete once all kills are made.

🔍 Investigation Quest

Visit specific locations and use Explore actions to gather clues. The required number of clues is shown on the card. Return to complete once all clues are found.

📦 Delivery / Errand

Travel from one location to another while "carrying" the quest item (represented by holding the card). Completing the journey completes the quest.

⭐ Major Contract

Multi-step quest combining monster kills, exploration, and specific location visits. These yield the highest VP rewards and take multiple turns to complete. Always worth pursuing.

Quest Rewards

Printed on the quest card. Rewards typically include: VP (the most common reward), gold, potion tokens, trophy tokens, or the ability to immediately draw a free card from the common row. Completing quests is one of the most efficient VP sources in the game.

16 Leveling Up

▼

How to Level Up

After defeating a monster whose level on its card is equal to or greater than your current witcher level, you may immediately advance your level marker one step (from 1 toward 5). You earn VP as shown on your character board for each level gained. You cannot gain more than one level from a single monster kill.

Choosing a Perk

When you level up, you choose one of two options shown on your character board for that level. The options and their exact effects are unique to each witcher character — read your character board carefully. Common perk categories include:

  • Increased maximum HP
  • Additional potion slot(s)
  • Increased hand limit (draw more cards per turn)
  • Unlocking a new mutagen slot
  • A unique character ability activating or upgrading
  • A free card gained immediately

Monster Level vs. Witcher Level

Each monster card shows its level (1–5). You can fight and defeat monsters of any level regardless of your current level, and you still earn the trophy and gold reward. However, only monsters at or above your witcher's current level count toward leveling up. As you progress, you must fight tougher monsters to continue gaining levels.

💡 The perk you pick at each level permanently shapes your witcher for the rest of the game. Think ahead — look at all four remaining perk choices on your board before deciding, so you build toward a synergistic set.

17 Meditation

▼

The Meditation Action

Spend 1 action to meditate. You may discard any number of cards from your current hand. For each card discarded this way, heal 1 HP (up to your maximum). Then resolve the meditation bonus shown on your character board — this bonus is unique to each witcher and is what makes meditation more than just healing.

Meditation Bonuses

Each witcher's character board shows their unique meditation bonus. These vary between characters and are one of the key differentiators in how each witcher plays. Refer to your character board for the exact bonus. Examples of the kinds of bonuses meditation can provide:

  • Drawing replacement cards from your deck
  • Gaining gold or potion tokens
  • Manipulating the common card row
  • Gaining a shield token that persists to your next combat
  • Sharing a benefit with another witcher at the same location

When to Meditate

Meditation is your primary healing source during the game. If you are significantly below maximum HP before attempting a difficult fight, always meditate first — even discarding good cards is usually better than entering combat weakened. The card you discard is gone only until your deck cycles; the HP you lose to a monster might knock you out and cost you gold.

💡 Weak starter cards you want to cycle out of your deck are perfect candidates for meditation discards — you get HP and thin your deck at the same time.

18 Mutagens

▼

Mutagens are permanent passive enhancements that modify your witcher for the rest of the game once equipped. They represent the alchemical augmentations that give witchers their extraordinary abilities.

Gaining Mutagens

Mutagen tokens are acquired through exploration cards, specific quest rewards, defeating certain monsters, some potion effects, and actions at witcher fortresses. You cannot equip a mutagen until you have an available mutagen slot on your character board.

Mutagen Slots

Mutagen slots are shown on your character board and are unlocked by leveling up — you start with no slots and gain them at specific level thresholds. When you gain a slot, you may immediately fill it with any mutagen token you hold. A mutagen placed in a slot is permanent — it cannot be removed or replaced.

Mutagen Effects

Each mutagen token shows its effect — these are passive bonuses that apply every combat round automatically without spending actions or cards. Common mutagen effect categories:

  • Combat mutagens — provide bonus sword (⚔) symbols each round
  • Defense mutagens — provide bonus shield (🛡) symbols each round
  • Vitality mutagens — provide healing at the start or end of each combat round

Exact values and any special conditions are printed on each individual mutagen token. There are also rarer mutagens with unique effects beyond the standard categories.

💡 Combat mutagens provide a flat bonus every single combat round for the rest of the game — even a small bonus like +1 ⚔ per round adds up enormously over a full game. Prioritize leveling up to unlock your first slot as early as possible.

19 Trophies

▼

Earning Trophies

When you defeat a monster (reduce its HP to 0), take the trophy token matching that monster's category. Each monster card shows which category it belongs to. Keep trophy tokens face-up in front of you — they are visible to all players throughout the game.

Trophy Scoring at Game End

At the end of the game, for each monster category: the witcher with the most trophies in that category earns bonus VP (shown on the trophy token or scoring track). In case of a tie, both players share the bonus (round down if indivisible). This scoring rewards specializing in certain monster types.

Mid-Game Trophy Trading

At certain notice-board locations, you may spend an action to trade a set number of trophy tokens for gold or immediate VP. Only worthwhile if you have a large surplus of one category and need resources urgently — end-game trophy scoring is usually worth more than early trading.

20 Witcher vs. Witcher Duels

▼

Initiating a Duel

If two witchers share a location, either may spend a Challenge Witcher action. The challenged player cannot refuse. A duel is resolved in a single combat round — both players simultaneously play cards and roll dice as they would in monster combat, but target each other.

1
Both players reveal their current hands to each other.
2
Both simultaneously play cards from their hands and roll any combat dice those cards require.
3
Each player's total ⚔ − opponent's total 🛡 = HP damage dealt to the opponent.
4
The witcher with more HP remaining after damage is applied wins. If tied, the challenger wins.

Duel Outcome

  • Winner: Gains 1 VP immediately and may take 1 gold coin from the loser
  • Loser: Takes the HP damage from the duel, loses 1 gold coin (if any), and if they were the challenger, their turn ends immediately
  • Both players discard their played duel cards and draw back up to hand limit normally at end of turn
💡 Duels are a secondary VP source for most witchers, but they are a key part of the Viper School witcher's strategy (expansion). For other schools, only duel when you have a strong hand and your opponent is low on HP — losing a duel wastes an action.

22 End Game & Final Scoring

▼

Game End Triggers

The game ends immediately at the end of the current round (all players finish their turns) when any of these conditions is met:

  • The monster trail is exhausted — no more monsters can be placed
  • A monster reaches the final space of the trail and triggers the end-game event
  • A player reaches or exceeds the VP threshold printed on the round tracker (varies by player count)

Final Scoring

  1. Each player notes their current VP total
  2. Check every trophy category — the player with the most in each category gains that category's bonus VP. Ties share the bonus (rounded down)
  3. Convert remaining gold to VP at the ratio printed on the board
  4. Add VP from any end-game card effects
  5. The player with the highest total VP wins

Tiebreaker Order

  1. Most total trophy tokens
  2. Most gold remaining
  3. Highest witcher level
  4. If still tied: share the victory

23 Solo Mode

▼

Playing Solo

The game supports solo play with a single witcher. Use the reduced board configuration shown in the setup rules for 1 player. The core rules remain identical — you take turns, fight monsters, buy cards, and complete quests alone.

Solo Victory Condition

In solo mode you win by reaching the solo VP target (printed separately on the tracker — it is lower than the multiplayer threshold) before the monster trail is exhausted. The challenge is optimizing your turns efficiently without competition for resources but with a harder time limit.

Solo Difficulty

Solo mode can be made harder or easier by adjusting the VP target, the number of location tiles used, or the starting resources. The rulebook provides suggestions for this. Solo is also the best way to learn the game — you can pause and re-read rules without holding up other players.

💡 Play your first game solo to learn how deck-building, combat, and the trail interact before adding the competitive layer of other players.

26 Glossary & Quick Reference

▼
Action Token
Spent to take one action. You have 2 per turn. Resets at start of each round.
Common Row
The 6 face-up cards available to all players to purchase with gold.
Deck Cycling
The natural process of playing through your deck and reshuffling your discard pile.
Deck Thinning
Permanently removing weak cards from your deck so your best cards appear more often.
Discard Pile
Cards played or discarded this turn. Shuffled back into your deck when your draw pile is empty.
First Player Token
Marks who goes first this round. Passes clockwise after each round's end phase.
Free Action
Does not cost an action token. Potions, certain card abilities, and character abilities can be free actions.
Hand Limit
Maximum cards you hold at end of turn (shown on character board). Excess must be discarded.
HP (Hit Points)
Your witcher's current health. Reaching 0 means you are knocked out.
Knocked Out
Reaching 0 HP — lose carried gold, reset to partial HP, return to nearest base location.
Level
Your witcher's progression tier (1–5), unlocked by defeating appropriately leveled monsters.
Monster Trail
The track where monsters appear each round and advance. Exhausting it ends the game.
Mutagen
A permanent passive bonus token equipped into a character board slot. Cannot be removed once placed.
Potion Slot
Space on your character board where you store a potion token. Max potions you can hold at once.
Reaction Card (Monster)
Drawn from the monster's own deck each combat round; defines the monster's attack and behavior that round.
Remove (card)
Permanently take a card out of the game — it never returns to your deck or discard pile.
Shield (🛡)
Blocks 1 point of incoming monster damage this combat round. Does not carry between rounds.
Sign
One of 5 witcher signs (Aard, Igni, Quen, Yrden, Axii) represented as card types in the board game.
Starter Deck
The 12 cards each witcher begins the game with. Unique to each character.
Sword (⚔)
Deals 1 point of damage to the monster this combat round.
Trophy Token
Earned by defeating a monster. Shows the monster's category. Used in end-game category scoring.
VP (Victory Points)
The scoring currency. Tracked openly. Player with most at game end wins.

⚡ Combat Quick Reference

Your damage dealt: Total ⚔ symbols (cards + dice)

Damage to you: Monster's attack − your 🛡 symbols

Flee penalty: HP loss shown on monster card

Knocked out: Lose carried gold, return to base, reset to partial HP

Win: Take trophy token + gold reward on monster card

🔄 Turn Checklist

1. Take 2 actions (any combination of: Travel / Explore / Fight / Train / Meditate / Quest / Duel)  →  2. Discard played cards & excess hand cards  →  3. Draw back up to hand limit from your personal deck
