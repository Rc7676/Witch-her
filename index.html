1 Game Overview & Objective

▼

The Witcher: Old World is a competitive adventure board game for 1–5 players (ages 14+, ~120–180 min). Designed by Łukasz Woźniak and published by Go On Board (2022). It is set during the Golden Age of Witchers — decades before the events of the video games or novels — when monster activity was far greater and multiple witcher schools were still active.

Objective

Each player controls a witcher competing to earn the most Victory Points (VP) by the time the game ends. VP are earned through:

  • Defeating monsters and collecting trophies
  • Completing quests and contracts
  • Leveling up your witcher
  • Earning gold (converted to VP at game end)
  • Winning witcher vs. witcher duels

Core Identity

The game blends deck-building (building your combat card deck), adventure (exploring the map, uncovering events), and role-playing elements (quests, character progression, leveling). Each witcher school plays differently, rewarding different strategies.

💡 Designer's Intent: While competitive, players share the same board — cooperation on the same monster is possible, but trophies go to whoever lands the killing blow.

2 Components

▼

Board & Tiles

  • 1 double-sided game board (The Continent map)
  • Modular location tiles (adjust board size by player count)
  • 1 monster trail track
  • 1 round / game-end tracker

Cards

  • 5 witcher starter decks (12 cards each)
  • Action / combat cards (common deck for purchasing)
  • Monster cards with ability decks
  • Event / exploration cards
  • Quest cards
  • Trail / special cards

Tokens & Markers

  • Gold coins (1 and 3 denominations)
  • Shield tokens (Defense)
  • Trophy tokens (per monster type)
  • Potion tokens (Swallow, Cat, Thunderbolt, Tawny Owl, White Honey, Blizzard, Full Moon)
  • Mutagen tokens (Lesser, Greater, Grand)
  • Action tokens (2 per player)
  • Witcher level markers
  • Elixir / charge tokens
  • Damage tokens

Miniatures & Standees

  • 5 witcher miniatures (or standees in base version)
  • Monster standees / miniatures

Dice

  • Combat dice (custom symbols: Sword, Shield, Dodge, Special)
  • Standard dice for certain event resolutions

3 Setup

▼
1
Board: Place the game board. Adjust which location tiles are included based on the player count (fewer tiles for 2 players, all for 5).
2
Monster Trail: Shuffle monster cards and place them face-down on the monster trail spaces. Reveal the first monster per instructions.
3
Common Card Row: Shuffle all purchasable action cards. Deal 6 face-up to form the common row. Place the rest as the draw pile.
4
Quests: Shuffle quest cards and deal a set number to quest slots on the board.
5
Choose Witchers: Each player picks a witcher character board. Take the matching miniature/standee, place it on the starting location (Kaer Morhen or your school's starting point).
6
Starting Decks: Each player takes their witcher's unique starter deck (12 cards), shuffles it, and draws a starting hand (usually 5 cards).
7
Starting Resources: Each player takes starting gold (usually 2 coins), 1 Swallow potion token, and places their level marker on Level 1 of their character board.
8
Round Marker: Place the round token on Round 1.
9
First Player: The player who most recently played a video game goes first (or use another method). Give them the first-player token.
💡 For your first game, use the suggested 3-player setup with the introductory side of the board and pre-selected quests.

4 Witcher Characters

▼

Each witcher has a unique character board, unique starter deck, and a special passive ability. Starting stats are generally the same (14 HP, Level 1) but card compositions differ significantly.

🐺

Geralt of Rivia

Wolf School
Passive: Before initiating a fight with a monster, Geralt may look at the top card of that monster's reaction deck, then put it back.
▶ Versatile all-rounder. Good starter witcher for new players. Balanced between combat, signs, and potions.
🐺

Vesemir

Wolf School
Passive: Vesemir starts with an extra trophy slot. When he meditations, he may give one card from his hand to another witcher at the same location.
▶ Support/mentor role. Best in multiplayer. Helps allies while earning trophies for himself.
🐺

Lambert

Wolf School
Passive: Lambert may spend 1 extra gold when purchasing cards — he draws 2 and keeps 1. His aggressive deck starts with extra sword cards.
▶ Aggressive brawler. High damage output. Struggles with healing. Best for experienced players who want pure combat.
🐺

Eskel

Wolf School
Passive: Eskel may reroll one combat die per combat for free. His starter deck has improved shield and reaction cards.
▶ Defensive fighter. Good survivability, excellent for prolonged fights. Steady VP earner.
🦅

Coen

Griffin School
Passive: Coen's sign cards cost 1 fewer gold to purchase. When he plays a sign card, he may draw 1 additional card from his deck.
▶ Signs specialist. Relies on magic-based combat. High burst damage with correct deck construction.

Expansion Witchers (notable)

  • Letho (Viper School): Assassin. Gains VP from defeating other witchers. Aggressive PvP strategy.
  • Milva: Archer. Ranged attacks can damage monsters at adjacent locations.
  • Ciri: Unique movement — can teleport between locations. Very fast VP accumulation.
  • Triss / Yennefer: Sorceress characters with magic-focused playstyles (from expansions).

5 Witcher Schools

▼

A witcher's school determines their combat style, the composition of their starter deck, and which trophy types they prioritize. Schools don't provide blanket stat bonuses — the difference is entirely in cards and character abilities.

🐺

Wolf School

The most balanced school. Starter decks include swords, shields, and one sign. Witchers: Geralt, Vesemir, Lambert, Eskel. Style: Adaptable — can specialize after Level 2.

🐱

Cat School

Fast and aggressive. Starter deck emphasizes attack cards, fewer shields. High damage potential but lower base defense. Style: Kill fast before taking damage.

🐻

Bear School

Tanky and durable. Heavy armor emphasis. Starter deck has many shield cards and powerful strikes but fewer cards overall. Style: Outlast enemies through endurance.

🦅

Griffin School

Signs-focused. Starter deck has more sign cards than any other school. Best synergy with magic-based cards. Style: Control and signs combo chains.

🐍

Viper School

Poison and assassination. Unique cards inflict ongoing damage (poison tokens on monsters). Style: Debilitate over multiple rounds. Also excels at PvP.

🦂

Manticore School

Alchemy specialists. Expanded potion slots and potion synergy cards. Potions provide stronger effects. Style: Preparation-heavy; set up before major fights.

6 Round Structure

▼

The game is played over a series of rounds. Each round consists of two phases:

Phase 1

Player Turns (clockwise)

Starting with the first player and going clockwise, each player takes their full turn before the next player goes. A turn consists of performing 2 actions (using your 2 action tokens). You may also perform free actions (playing cards that say "free action") at any time during your turn.

💡 You do not have to use both actions — but there is rarely a reason not to.
Phase 2

End of Round

After all players have taken their turns:

  • Advance the monster trail (monsters slide forward one space; if a monster reaches the end, the game may end or a penalty is triggered depending on variant)
  • Reveal a new monster to the end of the trail
  • Refresh the common card row (discard leftover cards if instructed, deal new ones)
  • Pass the first-player token clockwise
  • Advance the round marker
  • Each player resets their action tokens

End of Turn (after your 2 actions)

  • Discard all cards played this turn
  • Discard down to your hand limit (usually 3) — discard excess
  • Draw back up to your hand size from your personal deck
  • If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck

7 Actions

▼

On your turn you take exactly 2 actions. Each action token is spent on one of the following:

🚶

Travel

Move your witcher along a path to an adjacent location. You may play movement cards to travel further. Terrain type affects how many cards / gold you must spend (roads are free, mountains cost extra).

🔍

Explore

At certain locations, draw and resolve the top card of the exploration deck for that tile. Exploration cards trigger events, grant resources, reveal hidden encounters, or provide clues to quests.

🗡️

Fight a Monster

If there is a monster at your location (or you move to one), you may spend an action to initiate combat. This is the primary way to earn trophies and VP. See Section 11 for full combat rules.

📚

Train (Purchase a Card)

Spend gold equal to the card's cost to take one card from the common row into your discard pile. The row is immediately refilled from the draw pile. You may train multiple times per turn (one action each, or some cards allow free purchases).

🌙

Meditate

Spend an action to meditate. You may discard any number of cards from your hand, then heal HP equal to the number discarded (up to your maximum). You also gain the meditation benefit listed on your character board. See Section 17.

📜

Complete / Take a Quest

At certain locations, spend an action to accept a quest (placing the quest card in front of you) or to complete a quest you already hold (if requirements are met). Completing quests awards VP, gold, or cards.

⚔️

Challenge a Witcher

If another witcher is at your location, you may spend an action to challenge them to a duel. Both players participate in a modified combat. See Section 20.

Free Actions (no action token required)

  • Playing cards from your hand that say "Free Action" or "Reaction"
  • Drinking a potion you already hold (timing varies by potion)
  • Discarding a card for its discard benefit (if listed)
  • Using a witcher ability with a "⚡ free" symbol

9 Exploration

▼

When you take the Explore action at a valid location, draw the top card of that location's exploration deck and resolve it immediately.

Exploration Card Types

🎁 Discovery

Immediately gain a resource: coins, a trophy token, a potion, a mutagen, or cards.

⚡ Encounter

A mini-event resolves. Usually a skill test or choice. Examples: "Spend 2 gold or lose 2 HP", "Draw 2 cards and discard 1".

👹 Ambush

A monster appears! You must fight it immediately (it does not go on the trail). Defeating it grants a trophy but no trail VP.

📜 Clue

Advances an active quest. Collect enough clues to unlock quest completion requirements.

💡 Exploration is the main source of potions and mutagen fragments outside of specific city actions. Plan your routes to pass through exploration-rich terrain.

10 Training (Buying Cards)

▼

The Common Card Row

Six cards are always face-up in a central row. Each has a gold cost printed in the corner. To train, spend an action and pay the gold cost of the card you want. The card goes to your discard pile (not your hand). The row is immediately refilled.

Card Categories

TypeCostFunction
⚔️ Combat2–5 goldPlay during combat for sword/shield symbols or dice
🌀 Sign3–6 goldMagic effects during or outside combat
🛡️ Defense2–4 goldShields, dodge, and counter effects
🎯 Special4–8 goldUnique powerful effects, often one-time use
💰 Economy1–3 goldGenerate gold, draw extra cards, thin your deck
🗡️ Witcher5–9 goldWitcher-school-specific powerful cards (when available)

Deck Thinning

Some cards let you remove a card from your deck entirely (place it in a "removed" box). This is very powerful — a smaller, focused deck cycles faster and you draw your best cards more often. Prioritize removing weak starter cards early.

💡 The common row refreshes after each purchase, so you can sometimes buy multiple cards in one turn if you have enough gold-generating cards in hand.

11 Monster Combat

▼

Combat is the heart of the game — a tactical card-and-dice system where you must manage your deck against the monster's behavior deck.

1
Initiate: Spend an action to fight a monster at your location. Take the monster card — it shows HP, level, and which reaction deck to use.
2
Monster Reaction: Draw the top card from the monster's reaction deck. This card defines what the monster does this round (attack value, special ability, damage type).
3
Play Your Cards: Play cards from your hand face-up. Resolve their effects: some give sword icons (⚔), shield icons (🛡), dice rolls (🎲), sign effects, or special abilities.
4
Roll Dice: If you played cards that instruct you to roll dice, roll now. Add the results (sword, shield, special symbols) to your total from cards.
5
Resolve Damage:
  • Count your total ⚔ symbols → this is damage dealt to the monster
  • The monster deals its printed damage minus your total 🛡 shields
  • Any unblocked monster damage is dealt to your HP
6
Repeat: Combat continues for multiple rounds until the monster's HP reaches 0 (you win) or your HP reaches 0 (you flee or are defeated).
7
Resolution: If you defeat the monster, take the trophy token and any gold reward printed on the monster card. The monster is removed from the board.

⚔ Sword Symbols

Each ⚔ deals 1 damage to the monster. Some cards grant +2 or more. Swords from cards and dice combine.

🛡 Shield Symbols

Each 🛡 blocks 1 damage from the monster's attack this combat round. Shields do not carry over between rounds.

🎲 Dice Roll

Combat dice have custom faces: Sword, Shield, Dodge (no effect but can be re-rolled), and Special (per-card instructions). Roll results add to your card results.

❤ Monster HP

Monsters have HP listed on their card. Damage accumulates — you don't have to kill in one round. Place a damage marker on the monster between rounds.

Fleeing Combat

At the end of any combat round (before starting a new one), you may choose to flee. You lose 2 HP (minimum — some monsters may deal more), discard your hand, and your turn ends. The monster remains at the location at its current HP.

Being Defeated (0 HP)

If your HP drops to 0 mid-combat: you are knocked out. Lose all unspent gold you are carrying (place it on the board for others to collect), discard your hand, reset your HP to half maximum (rounded up), and return to the nearest friendly location (Kaer Morhen or your school's base). You do NOT lose cards from your deck.

💡 Monster Weakness: Each monster has a weakness printed on its card (e.g., Silver, Igni Sign, specific oil). Playing cards that match the weakness often triggers bonus effects or extra damage.

12 Card System (Deck Building)

▼

Your Personal Deck

Each witcher maintains their own personal deck of cards. You start with a 12-card starter deck and grow/improve it by purchasing cards. At any time your deck = starter cards still in deck + purchased cards still in deck (not counting removed or in discard/hand).

Hand Limit & Drawing

Your hand limit is printed on your character board (usually 3–4 cards). At the end of your turn, you discard all cards played and any excess hand cards, then draw back up to your hand limit. If your deck runs out mid-draw, immediately shuffle your discard pile into a new deck and continue drawing.

During Combat — Extra Draw

You may play all cards in your hand during combat (not limited to hand size). Some cards also let you draw additional cards during combat. This is the main way to output high damage in a single combat round.

Card Anatomy

13 Signs

▼

Signs are magical abilities unique to witchers. In the board game, signs are represented by cards you can purchase or that come with certain starter decks. Playing a sign card triggers its specific effect.

🌊

Aard

Telekinetic Strike. When played: the monster loses its next shield symbol (its defense is reduced for this round) or you may push a small monster to an adjacent location.

🔥

Igni

Fire Stream. When played: deal direct damage to the monster equal to the number printed on the card (bypasses its shields). Very effective against certain monster types.

🛡️

Quen

Magic Shield. When played: place 2–3 shield tokens on yourself. These persist into the next combat round (unlike regular card shields) and absorb damage before your HP is reduced.

🕸️

Yrden

Magic Trap. When played: the monster's next reaction card is revealed and you choose whether to resolve it or discard it and draw a new one. Powerful for avoiding nasty monster abilities.

💫

Axii

Charm / Confusion. When played: reduce the monster's attack value this round by a set amount, or (in witcher vs. witcher) force the opponent to discard a card from their hand.

💡 Sign cards bought from the common row are more powerful than starter sign cards. Griffin School witchers pay 1 less gold for sign cards — build around this advantage.

At a Witcher Fortress or city with an alchemist, spend ingredients (gained from exploration) and gold to craft specific potions. Crafted potions can be stored beyond your normal limit (Manticore School perk).

💡 Never enter a fight with fewer than 2 potions — Swallow for healing and one combat potion. Thunderbolt is almost always the best combat potion.

15 Quests & Contracts

▼

Quest Basics

Quest cards are placed at notice board locations on the map. To accept a quest, visit that location and spend an action. The quest card is placed in front of you. You may hold up to 2 quests at once (modified by level).

Quest Types

🗡️ Combat Contract

Defeat a specific monster type (e.g., "Defeat 2 Necrophage monsters"). When the condition is met, return to a notice board to collect the reward.

🌍 Exploration Quest

Visit a specific location and use the Explore action to find a clue or item. May require multiple exploration actions across different sites.

💰 Delivery Quest

Carry an item from one location to another. Straightforward but requires planning your route efficiently.

⭐ Epic Quest

Multi-step quest granting large VP rewards. Usually requires a combination of combat, exploration, and visits to multiple locations. Worth 5–8 VP on completion.

Rewards

Quest rewards are printed on the card and typically include: Victory Points (2–8 VP), Gold (2–5 coins), Trophy tokens, Potion tokens, or the right to immediately purchase a card from the row for free.

💡 Quests are one of the most efficient VP sources in the game. A witcher who completes 3 quests in a game has a significant advantage. Prioritize quests that align with your planned monster hunts.

16 Leveling Up

▼

How to Level Up

After defeating a monster of a level equal to or higher than your current witcher level, you may immediately level up. Advance your level marker on your character board (max Level 5 in base game). Leveling up happens once per fight — you cannot gain multiple levels from one kill.

Witcher Levels & Benefits

Each level unlocks new perks shown on your character board. You choose one of two options at each level. Typical choices:

LevelVP RewardTypical Perk Options
1 → 20 VP+2 Max HP OR +1 potion slot
2 → 31 VP+1 hand size OR gain a special card for free
3 → 42 VPUnlock witcher-specific ability OR +3 Max HP
4 → 53 VPPowerful unique ability OR +1 mutagen slot

Leveling & Monster Tiers

Monster difficulty is measured by level (1–5). Fighting higher-level monsters is riskier but the only way to advance beyond Level 1. You cannot level up from defeating a monster lower than your current level — though you still get the trophy and gold.

💡 Level 3 is the critical breakpoint — the perk at 3→4 is usually your character's defining ability. Plan your deck to synergize with it from the start.

17 Meditation

▼

Meditation Action

Spend 1 action. You may discard any number of cards from your current hand. For each card discarded, heal 1 HP (up to your maximum). Additionally, resolve the meditation bonus printed on your character board.

Common Meditation Bonuses (per character)

WitcherMeditation Bonus
GeraltDraw 2 cards, keep 1
VesemirGive 1 card to an ally at same location
LambertGain 1 gold
EskelGain 1 shield token (persists to next combat)
CoenLook at top 3 cards of common row, rearrange them

Enhanced Meditation (at Witcher Fortress)

When meditating at a Witcher Fortress location, you also get to: heal to full HP (regardless of cards discarded), craft 1 potion for free, or remove 1 card from your deck for free.

💡 Meditation is your main source of healing outside of Swallow potions. If you're below 50% HP before a major fight, always meditate first — discarding weak cards and healing is almost always worth the action.

18 Mutagens

▼

Mutagens are permanent passive bonuses that enhance your witcher's abilities. They represent the alchemical augmentations that transform humans into witchers.

Gaining Mutagens

Mutagen tokens are earned through: exploration cards, specific quest rewards, defeating certain monsters, the Full Moon potion, or spending actions at a Witcher Fortress. You start with 0 mutagen slots. Slots unlock at specific levels.

Mutagen Types

TypeSymbolEffect When Equipped
Lesser Mutagen🔵+1 ⚔ in every combat round
Lesser Mutagen🟢+1 🛡 in every combat round
Lesser Mutagen🔴Heal 1 HP at the start of each round in combat
Greater Mutagen🔵🔵+2 ⚔ in every combat round
Greater Mutagen🟢🟢+2 🛡 in every combat round
Greater Mutagen🔴🔴Heal 2 HP at start of each combat round
Grand Mutagen⭐Powerful unique effect per character

Equipping Mutagens

Your character board has mutagen slots (unlocked via leveling). Place a matching mutagen token in a slot — it is immediately active and remains for the rest of the game. You cannot remove a mutagen once equipped. Slots are unlocked at Levels 2, 3, and 4.

💡 Red mutagens (+healing per round) are extremely powerful for long fights against high-HP monsters. Blue mutagens are best for fast killing strategies.

Trophy VP (End-Game Scoring)

At game end: the witcher with the most trophies of each category earns bonus VP. Ties share the VP. Additionally, total trophy count may matter for certain quest or card effects throughout the game.

Mid-Game Trophy Redemption

At notice board locations, you may spend an action to trade 2 trophy tokens (of any type) for 1 VP or 3 gold. Only do this if you have a surplus of one category and VP is urgently needed.

20 Witcher vs. Witcher Duels

▼

Initiating a Duel

If you and another witcher are at the same location, you may spend an action to challenge them. The challenged witcher cannot refuse.

1
Both witchers reveal their current hands simultaneously.
2
Both players play cards for one round of combat (same rules as monster combat, but you deal damage to each other).
3
Both roll combat dice (if their played cards instruct it). Add results.
4
Each witcher's total ⚔ minus opponent's total 🛡 = HP lost by the opponent.
5
After one round: the witcher with more HP remaining wins. In a tie, the challenger wins.

Duel Rewards & Penalties

Winner: Gains 1 VP and may take 1 gold coin from the loser.
Loser: Loses 1 gold coin (if available), takes any damage sustained in the duel, and their turn ends immediately if they were the challenger.

💡 Dueling is rarely the most efficient VP source unless you're playing Letho (Viper School) who earns bonus VP from defeating other witchers. For most characters, monster hunting gives better returns.

VP Tracking

VP are tracked openly on your character board. There is no hidden VP — all players can see everyone's score at all times. This is intentional — it lets you know when to race ahead or when to block a leading witcher.

22 End Game & Final Scoring

▼

Game End Triggers

The game ends immediately at the end of a round when any one of these conditions is met:

  • The monster trail is exhausted (no more monsters to place)
  • A monster reaches the end of the trail and triggers the "trail end" event
  • A player reaches the VP target printed on the round track (varies by player count and game length)

Final Scoring Steps

  1. Each player counts current VP on their board
  2. Add VP for most trophies in each category (check all categories; ties split VP rounded down)
  3. Convert remaining gold at 3 gold → 1 VP
  4. Add VP from any cards that grant end-game VP
  5. The player with the most total VP wins!

Tiebreaker

In case of a tied VP total: the witcher with the most trophies wins. If still tied: most gold. If still tied: highest witcher level. If still tied: share the victory!

💡 Gold-to-VP conversion is inefficient — spend gold on cards and actions during the game. Only hoard gold if you're close to a 3-gold threshold near game end.

23 Solo & Cooperative Mode

▼

Solo Mode

Play as a single witcher against the monster trail. The game ends on the same triggers as multiplayer. You win by reaching the solo VP target (printed separately on the round tracker) before the trail ends. Reduced board size; harder monsters appear earlier on the trail.

Cooperative Mode (variant)

All players work together against an expanded monster trail with more powerful monsters. Instead of competing for VP, players share a collective goal: defeat a set number of trail monsters before the trail is exhausted. If you succeed, all players win together.

  • Players may freely share gold and potions at the same location
  • Two witchers at the same location can fight a monster together — each contributes cards and dice
  • Damage is split between participating witchers by mutual agreement
💡 For competitive play, solo mode is excellent for learning the game. Try it before your first multiplayer session — it teaches combat and deck building without the pressure of competition.

26 Glossary & Quick Reference

▼
Action Token
Spent to perform one action on your turn. You have 2 per turn.
Common Row
The 6 face-up purchasable cards available to all players.
Deck Cycling
The process of playing through your deck and reshuffling — happens naturally.
Discard Pile
Cards you've played or discarded this turn. Shuffled into new deck when needed.
Exploration Deck
Location-specific deck of event cards drawn when you Explore there.
First Player Token
Determines turn order; passes clockwise each round.
Hand Limit
Max cards in hand at end of turn (usually 3–4). Excess must be discarded.
HP (Hit Points)
Your witcher's current health. Reach 0 = knocked out.
Knocked Out
Reduced to 0 HP — lose gold, reset to half HP, return to nearest base.
Level
Your witcher's power tier (1–5). Higher level = better perks, can fight harder monsters.
Max HP
Your HP ceiling. Healing cannot exceed this value.
Monster Trail
The track where monsters appear and advance. Game ends when exhausted.
Mutagen
Permanent passive bonus token equipped to your character board.
Potion Slot
Max potions you can hold simultaneously (usually 3).
Reaction Card
A card (yours or monster's) played outside of your normal turn.
Remove (card)
Permanently remove a card from your deck — it goes out of the game entirely.
Shield (🛡)
Blocks 1 point of monster damage this combat round.
Sign
One of 5 witcher magic abilities (Aard, Igni, Quen, Yrden, Axii).
Starter Deck
Your 12 initial cards, unique to your witcher character.
Sword (⚔)
Deals 1 point of damage to the monster this combat round.
Trail Monster
A monster currently on the monster trail; defeating it grants bonus VP.
Trophy Token
Proof of a monster kill; used in end-game scoring by category.
VP (Victory Points)
The game's scoring currency. Most VP at game end wins.
Witcher Level
Advances when you defeat a monster ≥ your level. Grants perks and VP.

⚡ Quick Combat Reference

Your damage to monster: Total ⚔ symbols (cards + dice)

Monster damage to you: Monster attack − your 🛡 symbols

Flee cost: 2 HP minimum

Knocked out: Lose carried gold, reset HP to 50%, return to nearest base

Victory: Take trophy token + gold reward on monster card

🔄 Turn Summary

1. Take 2 actions (Travel / Explore / Fight / Train / Meditate / Quest / Duel)  →  2. Discard played cards + excess hand cards  →  3. Draw back up to hand limit

📊 Recommended Strategy by School

  • Wolf: Level fast → Level 4 perk → then hunt high-level monsters for 4–5 VP each
  • Cat: Rush trophies early, dominate 1–2 monster categories, sprint to end game
  • Bear: Take risky fights other witchers avoid (high HP = can survive) — tackle Level 4/5 monsters early
  • Griffin: Build sign deck, chain sign combos in combat, maximize card draw each turn
  • Viper: Focus on quests + PvP opportunities; use poison to weaken monsters for quick kills
  • Manticore: Stockpile potions, fight multiple times per area, use potions to compensate for weaker cards
